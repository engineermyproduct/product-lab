# Why I Started Product Lab

I’ve spent a number of years working in education, systems, and leadership, particularly in environments where structure, communication, and problem-solving sit at the centre of everything we do.

Along the way, I’ve also developed a growing interest in technology. Not just as a tool, but as something that can reshape how we learn, build, and work.

That intersection is where *Product Lab* comes from.

---

## Why Product Lab exists

Product Lab is my way of documenting the process of learning in public.

Rather than keeping ideas, experiments, and projects in the background, I want to:

- test ideas in real time  
- build practical, usable capability  
- learn by doing, and sometimes by failing  
- share the process, not just the outcome  

It’s less about presenting expertise and more about building it transparently.

---

## Current focus areas

At the moment, I’m exploring a few key areas:

- Cybersecurity fundamentals and applied thinking  
- AI workflows and automation tools  
- Digital systems and how they connect  
- Practical approaches to technical education and learning design  

These are not treated as separate silos. They overlap and inform each other.

---

## What comes next

This space will evolve through:

- short tutorials and breakdowns  
- experiments and prototypes  
- project logs and reflections  
- walkthroughs of tools, workflows, and ideas  

Some will work. Some won’t. That is part of the process.

---

## Closing thought

Product Lab is simply a way to turn curiosity into something structured and shareable.

If you are interested in learning, building, or experimenting in similar spaces, welcome along for the journey.
